Crispy Roasted Potatoes

Photo by Alpha Perspective on Unsplash

Description

An alkaline boiling step is the secret to these potatoes; boiling them with baking soda breaks down the surface pectin to create a starchy slurry that crisps up beautifully in the oven.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs Yukon Gold or Russet potatoes, peeled and cut into 1.5-inch chunks
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • 5 tbsp olive oil or melted beef tallow
  • 2 tbsp kosher salt (for the boiling water)
  • 1 tsp flaky sea salt and black pepper
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, finely chopped (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat and prep
    Oven to 450°F (230°C)

    Preheat your oven and place a large, rimmed baking sheet inside to heat up. Bring 2 quarts of water to a boil in a large pot.

  2. The alkaline boil
    10 minutes

    Add the kosher salt, baking soda, and potato chunks to the boiling water. Simmer for about 10 minutes until the exterior is tender but the centers are still firm.

  3. Rough up the surface
    Drain and shake

    Drain the potatoes and let them sit in the colander for 30 seconds to let the steam evaporate. Return them to the dry pot and shake it vigorously to rough up the outer edges into a starchy paste.

  4. Season and roast
    50 minutes

    Toss the potatoes with olive oil, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Carefully spread them onto the hot baking sheet in a single layer. Roast for 25 minutes, flip, and roast for another 20 to 25 minutes until deeply golden and shatteringly crisp.
